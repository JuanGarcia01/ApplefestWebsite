Recommended Shops and Restaurant

Check out some of the things you can do after spending your day at Ithaca Applefest!

Food vendors are not the only place to grab a bite to eat while you are at Apple Fest. Downtown is littered with award-winning restaurants of all types and price ranges. No street signifies this more than Aurora Street, often referred to as Ithaca’s Restaurant Row. Here, you can find all sorts of dining options and, if the weather is alright, a great selection of outdoor seating to enjoy an autumn afternoon or evening. If it's food you're looking for, here is a full list of Ithaca’s' restaurants.

Looking to unwind after the crowds and bustle of Apple Fest? Take a walk along Cayuga Lake’s lovely shore and enjoy the views of one of the Finger Lake’s greatest treasures.

If you are not already too full on sweets from Apple Fest, stop by Life’s So Sweet Chocolates for their wide array of candy options and homemade treats. Life’s So Sweet has goodies for young and old and is well worth the deviation from the Commons. 116 W Green St.

Stop by Ithaca’s historic Dewitt Mall, right off of the Commons, and peruse through new and antique books, historic postcards and Ithaca memorabilia, and locally made clothes and gifts at Dewitt’s local, colorful shops—all housed in a beautiful, historic edifice. 215 N Cayuga St.

Autumn Leaves Bookstore & Angry Mom Records: It’s no secret that the Commons has some of the best shopping in Ithaca. One of the Commons’ most unique shops, however, is Autumn Leaves Bookstore with its imposing selection of used books, and, right underneath it, Ithaca’s premier vinyl and CD shop, Angry Mom Records. Take a break from Apple Fest and detour through these two shops in one! 115 E State St.

Ithaca Coffee Company: Home of Gimme! Coffee and more, Ithaca is one of America’s most coffee-drenched towns. Give local roasts a shot while downtown. Stop by Ithaca Coffee Company for their large selection of locally made coffees (or, in the evening, for a beer at their tavern) or Press Cafe in Press Bay Alley to try another one of Ithaca’s local roasters, Copper Horse Roasters. Ithaca Coffee Company: 311 E. Green St. - Gateway Center Press Cafe: 118 W Green St
